On this page you'll find information to help you get started with STEEP to estimate the footprint of your commute.

How do I run STEEP?

I
Input your origin and departure destinations. Set the time of departure and indicate whether or not you would like to include traffic information in your search. By selecting the time window option you can see how various departure times affect your route and emissions. Additionally you can vary the car parameters to determine what effect different vehicles have on emissions. BootstrapTema

How do I interpret STEEP's results?

The emi
Emssions of the route are calculated based on the vehicle parameters that the user applies and the speeds derived from the calculated route. The vehicle parameters are passed on to a script which determines the CO2 emission factors (g/km) for the various speeds. The emission factors and equations are taken from EEA/EMEP Road Transport Handbook and Appendix 4 here, in which they utilize the COPERT model by Emisia here. BootstrapTema

What do the vehicle parameters mean?

Vehicl
Vehicle parameters are the main factor which determine the CO2 emissions. The vehicle standard can be found in the registration papers or in some cars on the side of your vehicle door. If that information is not available you can guess based on the year of manufacture for your vehicle based on the following table.
Emission Standard Year of Manufacture
Euro 4 2005 -2008
Euro 5 2009 -2013
Euro 6 up to 2016 2014 - 2016
Euro 6 2017-2019 2017-2019
Euro 6 2020+ 2020+
The vehicle emission standards are a set of maximum values for vehicle pollutants set by the European Comission to regulate pollution from transportation. For more information click here. BootstrapTema
