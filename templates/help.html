Help


On this page you'll find information to help you get started with STEEP to estimate the footprint of your commute.

How do I run STEEP?

STEEP can be run in a couple of simple steps:
  1. Input your origin and departure destinations
  2. Set the time of departure
  3. Indicate whether you want the 'eco' or the 'fastest' route
  4. Indicate whether you want to include traffic in the route calculation
  5. Specify your car parameters to make the emission calculation more accurate
  6. Click 'Calculate Emissions' and wait for the result to show on the map
Tip: By selecting the time window option you can see how various departure times affect your route and emissions.

How do I interpret STEEP's results?

Emissions of the route are calculated based on the vehicle parameters that the user applies and the speeds derived from the calculated route. The vehicle parameters are passed on to a script which determines the CO2 emission factors (g/km) for the various speeds. The emission factors and equations are taken from EEA/EMEP Road Transport Handbook and Appendix 4, in which they utilize the COPERT model by Emisia.

What do the vehicle parameters mean?

Vehicle parameters are the main factor which determine the CO2 emissions. STEEP uses three different vehicle parameters: fuel type, segment and (emission) standard.
Fuel Type

When fuel is burned by a vehicle engine it produces different amounts of CO2 emissions depending on the Carbon (C) and Hydrogen (H) ratio of the fuel. Additionally not all the fuel is converted to CO2 as the engine is not 100% efficient, this results in CO and other pollutants. Generally biodiesel emits the most CO2, followed by petrol and then diesel

Segment

The car segments influence CO2 emissions mainly through the weight of the vehicle. Heavier vehicles like SUV's require more energy and fuel to move and thus produce more CO2.

(Emission) Standard

The vehicle emission standards are a set of maximum values for vehicle pollutants set by the European Comission to regulate pollution from transportation. More information can be found here.

The emission standard can be found in the registration papers or in some cars on the side of your vehicle door. If that information is not available you can guess based on the year of manufacture for your vehicle based on the following table.

Emission Standard Year of Manufacture
Euro 4 2005 -2008
Euro 5 2009 -2013
Euro 6 up to 2016 2014 - 2016
Euro 6 2017-2019 2017-2019
Euro 6 2020+ 2020+
